Summary of my observations

The pictures above show results from four different map services for searched expression 'Vienna, Austria'. As you can see maps are zoomed to different scale and but the overall design is quite similar.

What do the services have in common?

These service are showing maps rendered in different ways, user can zoom in and out a move the map as he/she desires. There is possibility to change basemap to satelite or road map (mostly) and some additional layers. More over in all maps there is the tool for finding direction.

Which things are shown?

In every of these maps there are different basemaps, layers and functions. As a basemaps there are mostly road and satelite maps. Sometimes there are hybrid maps which are satelite maps with labels. In Open Street Browser user can choose between several OSM maps as a basemaps or he/she has possibility to display no basemap which is unique. Additional layers show traffic, transit, photos, weather, cycling paths, terrain, webcams or videos. In case of OpenStreet Browser user has the possibility to select his content from dozens of layers. Furthermore there is possibility of streetview and bird view (that is equal to 45Â°).

What do you like and dislike?

I like the possibility in OpenStreet Browser to not show any basemap but only selected layers with thematic content. However the basemaps are quite distracting from other content and they may be more generalized. In Bing maps I admire the possibility to make my one places without having an account and than export it to KML or GPX. Disadvantege of Bing maps is that in compared to Google maps they have street view only in big USA cities. Google maps have of course the biggest range of layers from all proprietary mapping services, specially I like the terrain and webcam layer because they are so uniqe. Finally special think in Yahoo maps which I like is the opportunity to share the map directly to Facebook, Twitter or Google+.
